What is Astrology?

Astrology is the study of the movements and relative positions of celestial bodies, interpreted as having an influence on human affairs and terrestrial events. This ancient practice has roots dating back thousands of years and continues to provide insights and guidance to millions around the world.

The origin of astrology can be traced to ancient civilizations such as Mesopotamia, Egypt, and India. It has evolved over millennia, incorporating various cultural and philosophical elements.

In Hindu tradition, astrology is mentioned in several Puranas, including the Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra and Brihat Samhita. These ancient texts form the foundation of Vedic astrology, providing detailed guidelines for astrological practices and interpretations.

Vedic Astrology

Vedic astrology, also known as Jyotish, is an ancient Indian system of astrology. It uses the sidereal zodiac and provides insights into various aspects of life, including personality, relationships, career, and spiritual growth.
